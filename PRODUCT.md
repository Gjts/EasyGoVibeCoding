# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

EasyGoVibeCoding serves Chinese-speaking beginners, developers, architects, technical leaders, and teams who need to learn, evaluate, and operationalize AI programming tools. They arrive with different levels of technical depth, but share a need for structured explanations, practical paths, and evidence they can trust when making learning or adoption decisions.

## Product Purpose

The platform turns fragmented AI-programming knowledge into a progressive, documentation-driven learning system. Its purpose is to help individuals and organizations understand mechanisms, choose tools deliberately, and build repeatable engineering capability. Success means readers can move from curiosity to informed action without confusing hype, paid placement, or planned features with verified guidance.

## Brand Personality

Engineering-minded, trustworthy, and clear. The voice is confident without pretending certainty, practical without becoming dry, and encouraging without treating AI tools as magic.

## Anti-references

- Opaque “AI magic” marketing that replaces mechanisms and evidence with hype.
- Generic developer-tool landing pages whose decoration overwhelms the learning content.
- Growth-hack marketplaces that disguise paid placement as editorial recommendation.
- Dense enterprise documentation that assumes every reader already knows the vocabulary.
- Visual or interaction patterns that trade accessibility, readability, or honest status for novelty.

## Design Principles

1. Teach the mechanism, then show the action: explanations should lead to a concrete next step.
2. Preserve editorial trust: commercial content must be unmistakably disclosed and structurally separate from teaching conclusions.
3. Support progressive depth: let beginners orient quickly while keeping enough rigor for experienced practitioners.
4. Make system status honest: distinguish implemented, partial, planned, sponsored, and externally verified information.
5. Prefer durable clarity over novelty: navigation, copy, forms, and feedback should remain understandable under real constraints and failure states.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA or better. All primary content and controls must support keyboard navigation, visible focus, semantic structure, meaningful labels, sufficient contrast, reduced-motion preferences, responsive zoom, and Simplified Chinese language context. Educational and commercial paths must remain understandable without relying on color alone.
